RTE Admission Karnataka 2026-27

March 2026 Updated March 2026 6 min read Bangalore, Karnataka

Every year, thousands of parents in Bangalore and Yelahanka look for information on RTE admission in Karnataka. The Right to Education (RTE) Act guarantees free and compulsory education to children aged 6–14 and reserves 25% of seats in private unaided schools for children from weaker sections. This guide explains who is eligible, what documents you need, and how to apply for RTE admission in Karnataka for 2026-27.

RTE applications for 2026-27 are typically open between February and April. Apply early through the Karnataka government's official school education portal.

What is RTE Admission?

The Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009 (RTE Act) mandates that all private unaided schools reserve 25% of their total seats in Class 1 (or entry-level class) for children from:

  • Economically Weaker Sections (EWS) — families with annual income below Rs. 3.5 lakh
  • Disadvantaged Groups — SC, ST, OBC, and differently-abled children

Under this provision, admitted children receive free education up to Class 8, with the school reimbursed by the government. This is a major opportunity for eligible families in Yelahanka and Bangalore to access private school education at no cost.

Who is Eligible for RTE Admission in Karnataka 2026-27?

To apply for RTE admission in Karnataka, your child and family must meet the following criteria:

  • Child must be between 6 and 7 years old as of June 1, 2026 (for Class 1 entry)
  • Family annual income must be below Rs. 3.5 lakh (for EWS category)
  • OR the child must belong to SC / ST / OBC / differently-abled category
  • Must reside within the neighbourhood school zone (usually 1–3 km from the school)
  • Child should not already be studying in a private school

Documents Required for RTE Admission in Karnataka

  • Child's birth certificate (original)
  • Aadhaar card of child and parent / guardian
  • Income certificate below Rs. 3.5 lakh (for EWS) — issued by Tehsildar
  • Caste certificate (for SC / ST / OBC applicants)
  • Disability certificate (for differently-abled children)
  • Address proof (Aadhaar / ration card / utility bill)
  • Recent passport size photographs of child

How to Apply for RTE Admission in Karnataka 2026-27

1

Check Eligibility

Confirm your child's age (6–7 years as of June 1, 2026) and that your family meets the income or category criteria.

2

Visit the Official Portal

Go to the Karnataka School Education department's official RTE admission portal. Registration is done online each year.

3

Fill the Application Form

Enter your child's details, parent/guardian information, and select up to 3 preferred schools near your residence in Yelahanka or Bangalore.

4

Upload Documents

Upload scanned copies of all required documents — birth certificate, income/caste certificate, Aadhaar, address proof, and photos.

5

Lottery and Allotment

If applications exceed seats, allotment is done by computerised lottery. Check results on the portal and complete school-level admission formalities.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is RTE admission in Karnataka?

RTE (Right to Education) admission is a scheme under which private unaided schools reserve 25% of their seats for children from economically weaker sections and disadvantaged groups. Admitted children receive free education up to Class 8, with the school reimbursed by the state government.

Who is eligible for RTE admission in Karnataka 2026-27?

Children aged 6–7 years from families with annual income below Rs. 3.5 lakh (EWS), or children belonging to SC, ST, OBC, or differently-abled categories, residing within the school's neighbourhood zone are eligible for RTE admission in Karnataka 2026-27.

How to apply for RTE admission in Karnataka 2026?

RTE admissions in Karnataka are done online through the Karnataka School Education department's official portal. Parents must register, fill the form, upload documents, and select preferred schools. Seats are allotted by computerised lottery if applications exceed availability.

What documents are needed for RTE admission in Bangalore?

Required documents: income certificate (below Rs. 3.5 lakh from Tehsildar), caste certificate (if applicable), child's birth certificate, Aadhaar card of child and parent, address proof, disability certificate (if applicable), and passport size photographs.

Is RTE admission available in Yelahanka schools?

Yes, eligible private unaided schools in Yelahanka participate in the RTE scheme. Applications are processed centrally through the Karnataka government portal, and seats in nearby schools are allotted based on residence and lottery.
