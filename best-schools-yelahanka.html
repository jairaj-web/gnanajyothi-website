Board Recognition

Qualified Teachers

Transparent Fees

No hidden charges — full fee breakdown from the school

Safety

Documents Required for School Admission in Yelahanka 2026-27

  • Child's birth certificate (original + photocopy)
  • Aadhaar card of child and parent/guardian
  • Transfer certificate from previous school (for Class 1 onwards)
  • Recent passport-size photographs (usually 4–6)
  • Address proof (Aadhaar, electricity bill, or rental agreement)
  • Previous year report card or progress report
  • Immunisation / vaccination records (for pre-primary)

Frequently Asked Questions — Schools in Yelahanka

Which is the best school in Yelahanka Bangalore?

Sri Gnanajyothi School (est. 1989) is the best affordable State Board school. For CBSE, Orchids International and Presidency School Bangalore North are highly rated. For international curricula, Canadian International School and Vidyashilp Academy are the top choices in Yelahanka.

How many schools are there in Yelahanka Bangalore?

There are over 90 schools in Yelahanka and Yelahanka New Town combined. This includes 20+ CBSE schools, 12+ Karnataka State Board schools, 3 international schools, and several ICSE schools. Yelahanka New Town has the highest concentration of newer schools.

What is the school fee range in Yelahanka 2026-27?

School fees vary widely: Karnataka State Board schools charge ₹8,000–₹25,000 per year. CBSE schools typically charge ₹40,000–₹1.5 lakh per year. International schools (IB/IGCSE) charge ₹2.5 lakh–₹10 lakh+ per year. Sri Gnanajyothi School offers one of the lowest fee structures in Yelahanka at ₹8,000–₹18,000 per year.

Which board is best for a school in Yelahanka?

Karnataka State Board (KSEEB) is best for families settled in Karnataka — affordable and ideal for local CET/NEET exams. CBSE is better for families who relocate frequently or prefer a national curriculum. IB/IGCSE suits families wanting internationally recognised education. ICSE is strong in English and Science depth.

Are school admissions open in Yelahanka for 2026-27?

Yes, admissions for 2026-27 are currently open at most Yelahanka schools. Popular schools fill their seats by March–April. Sri Gnanajyothi School is currently accepting admissions for LKG to Class 10. Call +91 90358 44103 to enquire or schedule a campus visit.

Which is the best affordable school in Yelahanka?

Sri Gnanajyothi School in Yelahanka Agrahara is the best affordable school in Yelahanka. Annual fees range from ₹8,000–₹18,000 with no hidden charges or donations required. The school has 37+ years of experience and offers English medium education from LKG to Class 10.

What documents are needed for school admission in Yelahanka?

Typically required: birth certificate, Aadhaar card (child + parent), transfer certificate from previous school (Class 1 onwards), passport photographs, address proof, previous year report card, and vaccination records for pre-primary admissions.

Is there a State Board English medium school in Yelahanka?

Yes. Sri Gnanajyothi School in Yelahanka Agrahara is a Government-recognised Karnataka State Board English medium school offering LKG to Class 10. Jnana Jyothi School in Yelahanka New Town is another State Board option in the area.

Which area in Yelahanka has the most schools?

Yelahanka New Town has the highest concentration of schools, particularly CBSE and international schools. Yelahanka Agrahara (Old Yelahanka) has well-established, affordable state board schools like Sri Gnanajyothi School. Kogilu and Bagalur Road areas also have growing school options.

How do I compare and choose the right school in Yelahanka?

Compare schools by: board affiliation (State/CBSE/ICSE/International), fee structure (total annual cost including all charges), student-teacher ratio, infrastructure (labs, library, sports), board exam results history, distance from home, and parent/student reviews. Always visit the campus before finalising admission.
