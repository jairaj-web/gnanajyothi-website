Age Criteria for Class 6 and Class 7 Admission in Karnataka

The Karnataka government (KSEEB / DDPI) has set age norms for all classes. For the 2026-27 academic year, children must meet the following minimum age as of June 1, 2026:

Schools may grant a relaxation of up to 6 months in age if the child has already passed the qualifying class from a recognised school. The final decision rests with the school principal and DDPI guidelines.

Is TC (Transfer Certificate) Mandatory for Class 6 Admission?

Yes. A Transfer Certificate from the previous school is compulsory for admission to Class 6 or Class 7 in any government-recognised school in Karnataka. Here is what you need to know about the TC:

  • TC must be issued by the principal of the previous school on official letterhead
  • If transferring within the same district — TC alone is sufficient
  • If transferring from another district — TC must be countersigned by the Block Education Officer (BEO) of the previous district
  • If transferring from another state — TC must be countersigned by the respective state's education authority
  • TC must mention the class last studied, date of leaving, and conduct certificate

Do not lose the original TC. Schools will ask for the original. A duplicate TC can be issued by the previous school but the process takes time.

Documents Required for Class 6 / Class 7 Admission in Yelahanka

Keep all these documents ready before visiting a school in Yelahanka for admission:

  • Transfer Certificate (TC) from previous school — original
  • Mark sheet / Progress Report of Class 5 (for Class 6 admission) or Class 6 (for Class 7 admission)
  • Child's birth certificate (original + photocopy)
  • Aadhaar card of the child
  • Aadhaar card of both parents / guardians
  • Recent passport size photographs of child (4–6 copies)
  • Address proof in Yelahanka (electricity bill / rental agreement / Aadhaar)
  • Caste certificate (if applying for SC/ST/OBC fee concession)
  • Income certificate (if applying for fee waiver or RTE benefit)

Can You Get Admission Mid-Year to Class 6 or Class 7?

Yes, mid-year admissions are possible in Yelahanka schools for Class 6 and Class 7, but they depend on seat availability. Here is how mid-year admission works:

  1. Contact the school — Confirm seat availability for the specific class before collecting documents.
  2. Get TC from previous school — Request TC as soon as the decision to transfer is made. Schools must issue TC within 7 working days by law.
  3. Collect current year progress report — The new school will need to see how your child has been performing in the current academic year.
  4. Submit application — Fill the new school's admission form with all documents.
  5. Principal approval — For mid-year transfer, the principal reviews the case and may interact with the child briefly.
  6. Pay fees and enroll — Once approved, pay the fees and collect the admission letter.

What to Look for When Choosing a Class 6 School in Yelahanka

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the age for Class 6 admission in Karnataka 2026-27?

For Class 6 admission in Karnataka 2026-27, the child must be at least 10 years of age as of June 1, 2026. Most KSEEB schools in Yelahanka strictly follow the Karnataka DDPI age norms.

Is TC required for Class 6 admission in Yelahanka?

Yes, a Transfer Certificate (TC) from the previous school is mandatory. If transferring from another district or state, the TC must be countersigned by the Block Education Officer (BEO).

Can I get Class 7 admission mid-year in Yelahanka?

Yes, mid-year admission to Class 7 is possible subject to seat availability. You need TC from the previous school, current year progress report, and all standard documents. Contact the school to confirm seat availability first.

What documents are needed for Class 6 admission in Bangalore?

TC from previous school, Class 5 mark sheet, birth certificate, Aadhaar card of child and parents, passport photos, address proof, and caste/income certificate if applicable.
