What is UKG?

UKG stands for Upper Kindergarten (also called Senior KG or Class 2 Pre-Primary). It is the second year of pre-primary education, after LKG. UKG prepares children for Class 1 by building on the foundation laid in LKG — with more structured learning, reading readiness, phonics, writing practice and early mathematical thinking.

In Karnataka, UKG is typically a one-year programme for children aged 4.5 to 5.5 years, followed by admission to Class 1 (Primary).

Documents Required for UKG Admission

Keep these documents ready before applying for UKG admission in Yelahanka:

  • Child's original birth certificate
  • LKG Transfer Certificate (TC) from previous school
  • LKG Progress Report / Report Card
  • Aadhaar card of child and parent/guardian
  • 4 recent passport size photographs of the child
  • Address proof (electricity bill, rental agreement or Aadhaar)
  • Caste certificate (if applicable for fee concessions)

UKG Admission Process — Step by Step

  1. Shortlist schools — Visit 2–3 schools in Yelahanka to compare facilities, teaching approach and fees
  2. Check age eligibility — Confirm your child meets the age criteria for UKG at your preferred school
  3. Collect application form — Get the admission form from the school office or enquire online
  4. Submit documents — Submit all required documents along with the filled application form
  5. School interaction — Some schools conduct a simple interaction with the child and parent. This is informal at UKG level
  6. Pay fees and confirm seat — Once selected, pay the admission fee to secure your child's seat for 2026-27

What to Look for in a UKG School in Yelahanka

1. Safe and Child-Friendly Environment

The school should feel safe, clean and welcoming. Check for CCTV coverage, safe playground equipment and well-maintained classrooms. At UKG level, the environment matters more than facilities.

2. Qualified Teachers

UKG teachers should have early childhood education training. A low student-to-teacher ratio (ideally under 25:1) ensures your child gets individual attention during this critical learning stage.

3. Play-Based Learning Approach

The best UKG schools balance structured learning with play. Children at age 4–5 learn best through stories, songs, activities and hands-on exploration — not just sitting at a desk.

4. Curriculum and Board

For parents in Yelahanka planning to continue in Karnataka, a Karnataka State Board school is a natural and affordable choice. The KSEEB pre-primary curriculum is well-designed and aligns with the NEP 2020 guidelines.

5. Proximity and Transport

For very young children, a school close to home reduces travel stress. Check if the school offers a van or bus service for your area of Yelahanka.
