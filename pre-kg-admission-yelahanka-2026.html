What is Pre-KG? Understanding Pre-Primary Classes

Pre-Primary education in India covers the years before formal schooling begins. In Yelahanka schools, you will commonly find the following class levels:

ClassAlso CalledAge (as of June 1)Focus
Pre-KGNursery, Play School2.5 – 3.5 yearsPlay-based socialisation
LKGLower Kindergarten, Junior KG3.5 – 4.5 yearsLetters, numbers, creativity
UKGUpper Kindergarten, Senior KG4.5 – 5.5 yearsReading readiness, Class 1 prep

Not all schools in Yelahanka offer a separate Pre-KG class. Many start from LKG directly. It is important to confirm with the school what their lowest entry point is.

Age Eligibility for Pre-KG & LKG in Yelahanka 2026-27

The Government of Karnataka has set minimum age requirements for Pre-Primary and Primary classes in line with NEP 2020. As of June 1, 2026:

  • Pre-KG / Nursery: Child must be at least 2.5 to 3 years old
  • LKG: Child must be at least 3.5 years old
  • UKG: Child must be at least 4.5 years old
  • Class 1: Child must be at least 5.5 to 6 years old

Private schools may have slightly different age policies, so always confirm directly with the school before applying.

Documents Required for Pre-KG Admission in Yelahanka

Most schools in Yelahanka require the following documents for Pre-KG or Nursery admission:

  • Child's birth certificate (original and photocopy)
  • Aadhaar card of child (if available) and parents
  • 2 to 4 recent passport size photographs of child
  • Address proof (electricity bill, rental agreement, or Aadhaar)
  • Health / vaccination record (optional but recommended)
  • Filled admission application form from the school

What to Look for in a Pre-KG School in Yelahanka

Choosing a Pre-KG school is a big decision. At this early age, the environment matters more than academics. Here is what every parent should check:

  • Safe, child-friendly campus — low furniture, padded corners, secured entry
  • Trained and caring teachers — Early childhood educators with experience
  • Small class sizes — Low student-to-teacher ratio for personal attention
  • Play-based learning — Structured play, storytelling, music and art
  • Hygiene and sanitation — Clean washrooms with child-friendly facilities
  • Proximity to home — A school close to Yelahanka Agrahara or YNT reduces travel stress
  • Smooth transition to LKG — School should offer LKG and UKG too for continuity

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the age for Pre-KG admission in Yelahanka?

For Pre-KG (Nursery) admission in Yelahanka, the child should typically be 2.5 to 3.5 years old as of June 1 of the admission year. Age policies can vary slightly between schools, so confirm with the school directly.

What is the difference between Pre-KG, LKG and UKG?

Pre-KG (Nursery) is for children aged 2.5–3.5 years and focuses on play and socialisation. LKG is for 3.5–4.5 year olds and introduces basic literacy. UKG is for 4.5–5.5 year olds and prepares children for Class 1 formal schooling.

What documents are needed for Pre-KG admission in Bangalore?

Typically required: child's birth certificate, Aadhaar card (child and parent), 2–4 passport size photographs, address proof, and the filled admission form. Some schools may also ask for a vaccination record.

Is Pre-KG compulsory before LKG?

No, Pre-KG is not compulsory. Children can join directly from home to LKG if they meet the age requirement. However, Pre-KG gives children a smooth, gentle transition to structured school life.
